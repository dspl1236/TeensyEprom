Signal Intercept Points

Injector Signal Intercept
ECU injector output wire is cut. ECU side connects to IRLZ44N drain. Source connects to injector. Teensy controls gate.

When Teensy gate is HIGH, injector fires normally. Teensy can shorten or extend the pulse by controlling gate timing relative to ECU signal.

Trim range: ~±30% pulse width
A flyback diode (1N4007) across each injector is required.
MAF Signal Synthesis
The stock MAF outputs a frequency signal (not analog voltage) on most VAG units of this era. Teensy reads the original MAF frequency on an interrupt pin, applies a correction factor based on MAP + wideband data, and outputs a corrected frequency to the ECU.

This "lies" to the ECU about airflow, effectively re-scaling the fueling for the larger 2.6L displacement.

Confirm MAF signal type (freq vs voltage) before implementing.
EPROM Bus Intercept
The original EPROM chip is removed from its socket. A ribbon cable connects the 28-pin socket to the level shifters, then to the Teensy FlexIO pins.

Teensy responds to every address read with data from its internal flash — effectively acting as a faster, smarter EPROM.

Access time target: <150ns — easily achievable at 600MHz.

Maps stored in Teensy flash can be updated over USB without opening ECU.
Knock sensor signal conditioning: The Bosch knock sensor outputs a small AC voltage centered around 0V. Teensy ADC cannot handle negative voltages. Add a 0.1µF coupling capacitor in series and a 100kΩ resistor to 1.65V (half of 3.3V using a voltage divider) to bias the signal into the safe 0–3.3V range. The Teensy's 600MHz M7 can then run FFT on this signal to isolate the knock frequency (~6–8kHz for these engines).
